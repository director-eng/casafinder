# Exhaustive Directory of Real Estate Websites Listing Properties in Ojochal, Puntarenas, Costa Rica

## Executive Summary

This report catalogs every identifiable website that lists properties for sale or rent in Ojochal, Puntarenas, Costa Rica. The directory is organized by website category to support Firecrawl-based data extraction, with full URLs, site descriptions, listing types, and crawl-relevant technical notes for each platform. A total of **52 distinct websites and platforms** were identified across 9 categories: local agencies, national brokerages, international franchises, MLS/portal sites, global aggregators, vacation rental platforms, property management companies, social media/classifieds, and related sister sites.

Costa Rica does not have a centralized MLS system, which means listings are fragmented across dozens of independent platforms with significant cross-posting between agents and agencies. This makes a multi-site scraping approach essential for comprehensive market coverage.[^1][^2]

### Key Technical Considerations

- **JavaScript rendering:** Airbnb, VRBO, and Booking.com require JS rendering for full content extraction. Firecrawl handles this, but expect slower crawl times.
- **Anti-bot measures:** Airbnb, Realtor.com, and Booking.com have robust anti-scraping protections. Use Firecrawl's stealth mode and rate limiting.
- **Cross-posting:** The same property often appears on 3-5+ sites simultaneously. Implement deduplication logic using property descriptions, GPS coordinates, or listing IDs.[^2]
- **Mixed languages:** Some sites (Encuentra24, Century 21 Ballena) mix Spanish and English content. Plan for bilingual content parsing.
- **No centralized MLS:** Costa Rica has no single MLS, though RE.cr (Propertyshelf) is the closest equivalent. Expect data inconsistencies across platforms.[^1][^49]
- **Pagination:** Most agency sites use standard `?page=N` pagination. Some (Osa Tropical, Costa Ballena) use infinite scroll or single long pages.

## Market Context

Ojochal's real estate market is dominated by foreign ownership, with 70-75% of properties in foreign (mostly corporate) hands. The market has experienced steady 6-10% annual appreciation, with vacation rental yields of 7-11%. Property prices predominantly fall in the $250,000–$750,000 range, though luxury estates regularly exceed $1.5M–$2.5M. The area has seen significant development across numbered phases (Phase 5, 6, 8, 9, 10, 11) and named neighborhoods like Calle Perezoso, Calle del Bosque, and Tres Rios.[^91][^5][^26]

Peak rental season runs December through April with 75%+ occupancy for well-managed properties. The market serves primarily North American expats (55%), Europeans (30%), and Costa Ricans (15%).[^80][^26]
